Why Wazuh Was the Only SIEM I Ever Needed

Joel Yang
Security Engineer
Feb 24, 2025
3 min read

When I first started working with SIEMs, I had zero experience with tools like Splunk, QRadar, or Sentinel. Wazuh — a free, open-source platform — became my gateway into log management, threat detection, and security automation. While commercial SIEMs dazzled with promises of "plug-and-play" features, Wazuh taught me the engineering behind security monitoring. Here's why it became my long-term solution.

Open Source = Full Control

Open-source flexibility eliminates vendor lock-in and provides full transparency in security monitoring. With Wazuh, I could inspect every rule, every decoder, every integration. No black boxes. No hidden costs. No surprise licensing changes that break your budget mid-year.

Cost? What Cost?

Being free means resources can be spent on hardware, training, and actually improving your security posture — rather than funnelling money into per-GB log ingestion fees or feature paywalls. For SMEs and startups, this is a game-changer.

Wazuh Taught Me Real Engineering

Manual rule-building and alert tuning forced a deeper understanding of SIEM fundamentals. Instead of clicking through a wizard, I was writing XML decoders, crafting custom rules, and understanding exactly why an alert fired. That knowledge transfers to any platform.

Scaling: On-Prem vs. Cloud Reality Check

Wazuh gave me the flexibility to deploy on physical hardware or cloud environments without scaling costs spiralling out of control. Whether it's a small lab or a multi-node cluster, the architecture adapts to your needs — not the other way around.

The Community Is the Secret Sauce

The Wazuh community and documentation are genuinely world-class for an open-source project. Instead of waiting on corporate support tickets, I found answers in Slack channels, GitHub issues, and community forums — often within hours.

Flaws? Sure. Dealbreakers? Never.

Is the setup complex? Yes — there's no one-click install. Does OpenSearch need tuning? Absolutely. Is there built-in AI-driven analytics? Not yet. But every one of those "flaws" taught me something valuable about security engineering. They're features in disguise.

"I didn't choose Wazuh over commercial tools — it just never let me down. It's flexible, free, and forces you to master the nuts and bolts of security engineering. Will I try Splunk or Sentinel someday? Maybe. But for now, Wazuh does it all."

What about you? Have you used Wazuh or other SIEMs? Share your wins, gripes, or questions below!
