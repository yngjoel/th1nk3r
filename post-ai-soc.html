AI Agents vs. Level-1 SOC Analysts: Augmentation, Automation, or Obsolescence?

Joel Yang
Security Engineer
Apr 27, 2025
3 min read

The security operations center is changing. AI agents are no longer science fiction — they're shipping as products. But does that mean Level-1 SOC analysts are heading for obsolescence? Let's break it down.

The Modern L1 SOC Analyst: Roles & Realities

Level-1 SOC Analysts are the first responders in cyber defense. Their daily grind includes:

  • Alert Triage & Enrichment: Reviewing SIEM/EDR/NDR alerts, adding context, assigning severity.
  • Initial Investigation: Running queries, extracting IOCs, escalating true positives.
  • Documentation & Handoff: Drafting clear incident briefs for Tier-2/Tier-3 teams.

The pain point? Alert overload. Up to 30% of alerts are never addressed, fuelling fatigue and burnout.

AI SOC Agents: The New Teammate

Agentic AI isn't just a chatbot; it's a reasoning engine that can interact with security tools. Key capabilities:

  • Autonomous Reasoning: Not just matching signatures; analyzing behaviour.
  • Tools Integration: Connecting with APIs to gather data and execute containment.
  • Scalability: Processing thousands of alerts simultaneously.

Comparing the Two

Capability Human Analyst AI SOC Agent
Context & Nuance Reads between the lines, spots motives Pattern-based; misses edge cases
Speed & Scale Limited by fatigue and shift coverage Milliseconds per alert, 24/7 uptime
Adaptability Tweaks playbooks on the fly Requires retraining or rule updates
Consistency Varies by experience and workload Uniform execution, zero drift
Compliance & Audit Human-verifiable decision trails Explainability improving but nascent

"AI handles the grind; humans bring the brain. Blend both to crush SOC objectives."

The Verdict: Augmentation, Not Replacement (Yet)

The future belongs to the "Centaur Analyst" — a human augmented by AI agents. AI handles the 80% noise, allowing humans to tackle the critical 20%. The result? Faster mean time to respond, fewer missed alerts, and analysts who actually get to do the interesting work.

Modern AI SOC Solutions to Watch

  • Prophet Security: Vendor-agnostic Autonomous SOC generating transparent Python playbooks, visual link analysis, and guided remediation.
  • Intezer: Automated triage and evidence collection for endpoint and cloud alerts.
  • D3 Security: Smart SOAR with integrated AI for playbook automation.

Human instincts or AI horsepower — what's your go-to SOC combo?
