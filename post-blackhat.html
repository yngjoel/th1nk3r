What I Learned About Kali Purple Hub, MALCOLM, and Autopilot at Black Hat Asia 2025

Joel Yang
Security Engineer
Apr 9, 2025
4 min read

I recently had the chance to attend Black Hat Asia 2025 in Singapore. While it wasn't the largest con out there, it turned out to be one of the more insightful ones — especially for blue teamers like myself.

What stood out most wasn't the vendor booths or CISO panels, but a hands-on Kali Purple Workshop that showcased something truly game-changing for defensive security education: a full-blown SOC simulation in a box — powered by Kali Purple Hub, MALCOLM, and Autopilot.

Kali Purple Hub: A Playground for Defenders

One of the presenters from Offensive Security made a striking point:

"Companies spend millions building SIEM infrastructure. But where do you go if you just want to learn?"

Kali Purple Hub solves that problem. It offers a fully preconfigured defensive lab where you can:

  • Analyze attacks end-to-end (from reconnaissance to exfiltration).
  • Trigger real-time alerts and detections.
  • Explore threat hunting workflows.
  • Understand how correlation, dashboards, and triage work in a real SOC.

Dashboards That Actually Mean Something

One of the most satisfying parts? Building dashboards that aren't just cosmetic. In Kali Purple Hub, you can:

  • Create visualizations based on Suricata alerts, Zeek logs, or Wazuh telemetry.
  • Map your detections directly to the MITRE ATT&CK framework.
  • See how a single network event propagates across different layers of the stack.

Incident Alerts & Case Workflow

The hub doesn't just dump logs; it teaches incident workflow. You're not just looking at a "Critical" alert; you're learning how to:

  • Triage: Is this a false positive or a legitimate threat?
  • Investigate: What did the attacker do before and after this alert?
  • Remediate: What steps are needed to contain the threat?

Optional SIEM Swap: Wazuh as a Drop-in

While the default stack often highlights Elastic, the workshop emphasized the modularity of Kali Purple. Wazuh is often the "sweet spot" for many practitioners because of its built-in FIM, active response, and regulatory compliance modules (like PCI DSS). Just note: Wazuh needs fine-tuning to fully slot into the Kali Purple ecosystem.

MALCOLM + Arkime: Network Telemetry That Goes Deep

If Kali Purple Hub is the brain, MALCOLM (and Arkime) is the nervous system. It provides a powerful, open-source network traffic analysis toolset, including session data, file extraction from network streams, and protocol analysis. It's the kind of deep-dive visibility you usually only get in high-end enterprise NDR solutions.

Kali Autopilot: Simulated Attacks Done Right

Detection is useless if you have nothing to detect. Kali Autopilot lets you simulate realistic attack traffic without manually running exploits every time. It generates the kind of messy, realistic traffic that actually tests an analyst's ability to distinguish between a routine admin task and a lateral movement attempt.

Extending Kali Purple

The project is modular, Docker-based, and designed for learning. Want to add Velociraptor for endpoint forensics? Shuffle for SOAR workflows? Grafana for custom telemetry? Go ahead — it's built to grow with you.

"You don't need to work at a Fortune 500 to learn SIEM engineering. You don't need a data center to practice threat hunting. You just need the right tools and curiosity to dig in."
